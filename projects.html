Worm Adventure 4: Into the Wormhole  (2021, Unity)

Worm Adventure 4: Into the Wormhole is my proudest game to date and is currently available on Steam after 14 months of solo-development and another couple months after launch fixing bugs and tweaking level design based on real user feedback. It was my first project in Unity and started out as a parody of my first ever game, Worm Adventure 2, but it was just so fun to play and work on that I couldn't stop making it bigger and bigger until I had a commercial game on my hands. Most of the time invested went into learning Unity, designing levels, and tweaking the character controller until it felt perfect. The grappling hook was implemented on the second week of development but was completely rewritten from scratch 6 times over the next year until I got something that worked the way I wanted.

Dice of Life  (2022, Unity)

Dice of Life began as an idea for an artificial life simulation like John Conway's Game of Life where the organisms are all six-sided dice which would perform different actions based on which face they rolled. So, when the theme for the Game Maker's Toolkit 2022 Game Jam was announced to be Roll of the Dice, I instantly knew what I was going to do. A simulation without a win condition, lose condition, or really any player interaction at all wouldn't perform well in a game jam, so I took my original idea and turned it into a puzzle game where you have to build the dice yourself instead of having them evolve, and it lead to some really cool immergent mechanics that I was able to make into puzzles. There are some levels that could have used more playtesting and a lot of mechanics were left on the cutting room floor, but I'm overall satisfied with the result.

Clean Up Fiasco  (2022, Unity)

Clean Up Fiasco is my most recent project and was my first time working in a team larger than 2 on a game jam. All three of us had slightly different ideas of what the game was going to be, so the design phase of development was challenging, but over a couple hours of discussion and design doc-ing we were able to reach a middle ground that we were all happy with, and came up with an idea that none of us would have come up with individually. This had me out of my comfort zone a little bit working on something I don't think I would have made on my own, so it was a great learning experience. I primarily worked on the dirt system, which utilizes Unity's DrawMeshInstanced function to draw tens of thousands of interactable dirt particles on screen, which all get stored in a data structure I put together in order to be able to randomly access any particle in constant time while keeping memory overhead low. This is what took most of my time, but I also worked on enemy AI, environment art, sound design, and menus.

Croquet Climb  (2022, Unity)

Croquet Climb was my submission to Ludwig's Foddian Game Jam and I had the idea of a game where you try to roll a ball up a hill. In order to prevent the player from being super safe and going really slow, which isn't any fun, I added a shot limit to encourage the player to take risks. The problem was then the question of what to do when the player runs out of shots. In the original version I submitted, the game ended when you ran out of shots, but that turned out to be a poor decision. Sending the player back to the start was punishing them based on their past mistakes which doesn't feel fair to the player and can leave them in a situation where they purposely restart because their run is hopeless. To try to solve this, I updated the game to make you take an uncontrollable shot when you run out, which still punishes the player for playing safe, but gives the player control over how much progress they lose when they run out of shots and decreases the influence of their past actions as long as they can perform well in the current situation.

Galaxy Trucktris  (2019, Gamemaker Studio 2)

Galaxy Trucktris was the first game I made after leaving for college and joining the game development club there, and is also non-coincidentally the last game I made in Gamemaker before making the leap to Unity. The theme for the jam was simply "retro" so I decided to try to take my own spin on the classic game Tetris by combining it with a board game I really love called Galaxy Trucker. Tetris and Galaxy Trucker are both tried and true fun, and they meshed together really well since the randomness and time pressure of Tetris fullfils the exact same purpose as the pile of parts and hourglass in Galaxy Trucker, so it was basically guaranteed to be fun. Even though I was still pretty inexperienced back when I made this game, it's still one of my go-to games to show at showcases since everyone instantly knows how to play because everyone knows Tetris.
